for this project, I get my inspiration from polar coordinates. As you see, I planed to do all rotation in polar coordinates, I even planned to do projection by polar coordinates. I began to have such idea just after I learn about polar coordinate in my precalculus class. My original plan is to do three plane rotation by adding the angles to polar coordinates, and do projection by dividing the magnitude of polar coordinate(of the plane x,y) by z to show the depth of each points.





Although the logic seems easier for man to understand, this is a complex logic for computer to calculate. Therefore, I and my classmates formulated a 2d rotation formular:

new\_x=x\*ti.cos(theta\_rad)-y\*ti.sin(theta\_rad)

new\_y=x\*ti.sin(theta\_rad)+y\*ti.cos(theta\_rad)

which is easier for computer.

I also began to realize that x/z and y/z actually has the same effect for polar\_magnitude/z.

Now, my project can finally be completely out of polar coordinates. But that does not mean I am out of inspiration.
